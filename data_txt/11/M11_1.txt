"We don't buy any thing here," said he, suddenly looking very indifferent, "this is a place where people pawn things." Pawn being a word I had never heard before, I asked him what it meant; when he replied, that when people wanted any money, they came to him with their fowling-pieces, and got one third its value, and then left the fowling-piece there, until they were able to pay back the money. What a benevolent little old man, this must be, thought I, and how very obliging. "And pray," said I, "how much will you let me have for my gun, by way of a pawn?" "Well, I suppose it's worth six dollars, and seeing you're a boy, I'll let you have three dollars upon it"
